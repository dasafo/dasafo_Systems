# 🧠 SKILL: Structured System Design (SSD) Orchestration

Based on the [bmad-orchestrator SSD](https://skills.sh/supercent-io/skills-template/bmad-orchestrator) standard.

## 🔄 The TEA Cycle (Internal Loop)
Within every phase of the `UNIVERSAL_PIPELINE` (M1 to M5), the Orchestrator must execute a TEA loop:

1. **T → Task (Decomposition):** 
   - Decompose the current phase into concrete atomic tasks.
   - Assign agents from the `AGENT_REGISTRY.md`.
2. **E → Execute (Multi-Agent Parallelism):**
   - Run tasks in parallel where dependencies allow.
3. **A → Architect (Validation):** 
   - Validate outputs for coherence and cross-phase traceability.
   - **Verdict:** Emit a `PASS`, `PASS_WITH_WARNINGS`, `REVISE`, or `FAIL`.

## 🚪 Phase Review Gates
- **Requirement:** No phase N (e.g., M2: Architecture) can advance to phase N+1 (M3: Execution) until a `PASS` verdict is generated by the internal Architect check.
- **Traceability:** Maintain a `$TARGET_PROJECT/LOCAL_KNOWLEDGE/ssd-state.json` to track the status of these internal gates.

## 📋 Per-Phase Focus
- **Phase 1 (Analysis):** Focus on discovery, analyst agents, and research scientists.
- **Phase 2 (Planning):** Focus on testers, designers, and planners.
- **Phase 3 (Solutioning):** Focus on security-reviewers and architecture critics.
- **Phase 4 (Implementation):** Focus on executors, code-reviewers, and build-fixers (Docker Proof-of-Build).
